So, why is Climate Change a problem?

Climate change is a long-term shift in global or regional climate patterns. Often climate change refers specifically to the rise in global temperatures from the mid-20th century to present.

As non-critical as this situation may seem, Climate Change indeed, is the most significant problem to be solved. 

We say so, because there's no actual, fixed definition of Climate Change. Every solution scientists have figured out till date, will have irreversible effects on Earth, which, we probably don't want. Not a lot of people really want to step up for the task, because trying to solve this problem improperly might backfire, and the problem-solvers themselves might get under the crosshairs. It is a mixture of several other environmental problems, and no two stakeholders normally share the same views for climate change, because the loss of a country might result in the win of another. The worst part, climate change affects everything. 

Hence, the best cure for this, is prevention.
